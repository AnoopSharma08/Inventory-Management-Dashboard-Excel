#Inventory Management Dashboard (Excel + Power Pivot)
#Project Summary
This project presents an interactive Inventory Management Dashboard developed using Microsoft Excel, Power Query, and Power Pivot to provide clear visibility into stock movement, inventory levels, and replenishment needs across multiple stores.
The dashboard transforms raw inventory data into actionable insights, enabling efficient monitoring and decision-making. It tracks key metrics such as total stock received (4,540 units), total stock dispatched (2,827 units), and current inventory levels (1,713 units). Additionally, it provides an overall stock availability indicator of 38%, offering a quick snapshot of inventory health.
A dynamic monthly trend analysis helps identify patterns in stock movement, allowing better planning and forecasting. The dashboard also includes a Bottom 10 low-stock report, which highlights products that are below their reorder levels, helping prevent stockouts and ensuring timely replenishment.
Interactive filters allow users to analyze data at both store and product levels, making the dashboard flexible and user-friendly for operational and managerial use.
#Objectives
•	To monitor inventory movement and stock levels in real time
•	To identify low-stock items and improve replenishment planning
•	To provide clear and actionable insights for decision-making
•	To reduce manual tracking and reporting efforts
#Key Features
•	Inventory tracking (Received vs Dispatched vs Available)
•	Monthly trend analysis for stock movement
•	Low-stock identification based on reorder levels
•	KPI indicators for quick performance evaluation
•	Dynamic filtering by store and product
#Tools & Technologies Used
•	Microsoft Excel
•	Power Query (Data Cleaning and Transformation)
•	Power Pivot (Data Modeling and Relationships)
•	DAX (KPI Calculations and Measures)
#Business Value
This dashboard helps organizations improve inventory visibility, reduce stock shortages, and make data-driven decisions. It minimizes manual effort and enhances operational efficiency, making it valuable for roles in data analytics, business intelligence, and operations management.
